High School Liaisons

The HSL program is an educational program partnered with NYCAASC. Join high school youth all over the city for weekly educational workshops and be part of planning for our annual conference!

Learn first-hand how a grassroots conference is put together while learning a variety of skills to advance your leadership and understanding of Asian/Pacific/American identity.

You can expect to...

  • Participate in weekly HSL workshops and join conference planning committees
  • Learn with mentors and peers through critical conversations about race, gender, politics, and social change
  • Develop organizing, communication, and leadership skills
  • Learn about Asian Pacific American histories, issues, and perspectives

HSL at NYCAASC 2017

This year’s program was anchored around 2 core questions:

  1. What does it mean to identify or not identify as "Asian American"?
    • What is the responsibility of Asian America?
    • What is/ was this term used to accomplish? Is this term still useful?
  2. What relationship does creative work have with our daily lives and politics?

Our group of 16 youth produced creative work along these themes, including a patchwork quilt, group poems, mini zines, and oral storytelling.

Asian American Identity Video Series

As part of their culminating project, our HSLs produced a video series following a day in their lives. Their short vignette style clips tangle with questions of what an “Asian American” life can look like from their various intersecting identities as students, as New Yorkers, as community members, and more. This was presented at the NYCAASC conference in an interactive art gallery and at the Charles B. Wang Teen Resource Center Open Mic in May 2017.
