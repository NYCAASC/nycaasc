The Eleventh Annual NYCAASC: REALITIES

2016 was an extremely divisive year - it revealed how little we truly know of our neighbors, even in New York City.

Our city is segregated by our various identities, like race and class. Although we share a single city border, we hardly know our neighbors beyond appearances and preconceived notions.

This year’s conference will explore the theme of REALITIES -- By delving into the experiences, histories, and communities of our families and neighbors, NYCAASC 2017 aims to identify and question the power structures that shape our realities.

  • What determines our realities?
  • Which realities are considered “authentic”?
  • And most importantly, whose realities become history?

Through the power of communal storytelling, REALITIES invites attendees to challenge our perceived realities of one another and to mobilize our communities beyond borders.

Speakers and workshop information will be released in April, so stay tuned!

Frequently Asked Questions

  • What is NYCAASC?

    The New York City Asian American Student Conference (NYCAASC) is NYC’s premier conference for exposing bright, young minds to issues that affect the Asian/Pacific Islander American community.

  • Where are we?

    We wouldn’t be NYCAASC without the NYC. The conference takes place at the NYU Kimmel Center for Student Life, which is located near the following subway stations: West 4 St - Washington Square (accessible via the A, B, C, D, F, & M), Astor Place (6), and 8 St - NYU (R and W).

  • When is NYCAASC 2017 happening?

    Mark your calendars for April 29, 2017! The conference is all day long, from 8:30 AM to 6:00 PM.

  • I’m not an Asian/Pacific Islander American. Can I still attend the conference?

    NYCAASC is run by APIA students, but is open to anyone interested in learning about Asian America, regardless of student status, race, or ethnicity!

  • How do I register?

    Click here to RSVP for the conference on Eventbrite. Just select the number of tickets you want, fill out your information, and you’re good to go!

  • How much is the conference?

    It’s FREE!

  • Really? I don’t have to pay anything?

    Really really.

  • Will there be food?

    YES! Broke college students, have no fear. We provide FREE breakfast, FREE lunch, and FREE snacks and refreshments!

  • Does NYCAASC host events other than the main conference in April?

    We have hosted a variety of satellite events alongside fellow APIA student clubs in the past, including open mics with a variety of headliners.

    Currently, there are no events planned for the 2017 school year but we hope to continue to hold some in the future.

  • How can I support NYCAASC?

    The easiest way to support us is to just attend our conference! If you, for some reason, can’t make it to NYCAASC, you can always like and share our event on Facebook, Twitter, and Instagram to help us spread the word about our conference; our conference’s longevity and success are dependent on YOU!
