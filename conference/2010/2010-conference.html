NYCAASC: Checkpoints

This April, the New York City Asian American Student Conference celebrates its 10th Anniversary with Checkpoints: Communicating Consciousness.

Join us on 4/23 at the NYU Kimmel Center to discuss A/P/A issues and reflect upon our history, action and inactions.

Change in Motion

Change in Motion intends to propel the established momentum of furthering an Asian American presence. Forces that interact with the notion of being Asian American radiate from numerous origins, and deeper analyses of these influences require deconstruction that is tenacious yet mercurial.

Keynote Speaker + Performances

Miriam W. Yeung, Keynote Speaker

Miriam W. Yeung is the executive director of the National Asian Pacific American Women’s Forum (NAPAWF), the only national, multi-issue Asian and Pacific Islander women’s organization in the country. NAPAWF’s mission is to build a movement to advance social justice and human rights for APA women and girls and does so through grassroots community organizing and public policy advocacy. Currently, NAPAWF is engaged campaigns for reproductive justice, immigration reform, and human rights for human trafficking survivors.

Prior to this Miriam was the Director of Public Policy & Government Relations at the NYC LGBT Community Center. Miriam serves on the board of Generations Ahead and Queers for Economic Justice.

She received her MPA from Baruch College and her BA from NYU.
